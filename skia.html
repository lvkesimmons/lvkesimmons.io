SKIA

skier protection system consisting of an action camera and an app

14 weeks

PROBLEM

Skiing is a popular sport around the world, but it can also be very dangerous.

If a skier goes down, it is often hard to pinpoint their exact location. It is even harder if there has been an avalanche. Experienced skiers often go down the hardest: trying more difficult runs and doing bigger tricks can result in worse injuries.

SOLUTION

SKIA uses an accelerometer and gyroscope to track the users G force, watching for crashes or falls.

SKIA is also constantly streaming to the cloud where the feed is monitored with image recognition.

Color options let skiers match the camera to the rest of their gear.

APP

Designed as a ski companion, the app tracks motion data and creates a social network.

The most important data is visible within a single tap or swipe.

Users were able to test the app prototype to give me usability feedback.

PROCESS

My original concept was to block off the space where tree wells form, but this was deemed impractical due to the sheer number of trees on the mountain.

Modeling both physcially and digitally brought out an aerodynamic form.
